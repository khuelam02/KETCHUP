01 — About

A Sauce
That Conquered
the World

The earliest known form of Ketchup was a fermented fish sauce from southern China. It made its way to Malaysia and Indonesia where it became kecap (pronounced "kay-chap"). That word evolved into ketchup in English. Originally, recipes used egg whites, mushrooms, oysters, grapes, mussels, or walnuts, among other ingredients, but now the unmodified term usually refers to tomato ketchup.

02 — History

A Centuries-Long Journey

18th Century

Early Recipes

Ketchup has a fascinating history that spans the globe. Various recipes for ketchup were published in the 18th century, with ingredients including mushrooms, oysters, and fruits.

19th Century

Rise to Fame

It was in the 19th century that tomato ketchup began its rise to condiment fame. It was popularized in the United States by entrepreneurs such as Henry J. Heinz and quickly became a staple in homes and restaurants alike.

03 — Variants

Beyond the Bottle

01

Tomato

The classic. Ripe tomatoes, vinegar, sugar, and spice — cooked low and slow into the sauce that defined a category.

02

Mushroom

A traditional British condiment predating tomato ketchup. Salted mushrooms fermented into a dark, umami-rich sauce.

03

Banana

The Philippine national condiment — banana ketchup was invented during WWII when tomatoes were scarce. Bright, fruity, and sweet.

04

Spicy

Chilli and pepper-infused ketchup. A global favourite for those who want heat alongside the tang. Found in fast-food chains worldwide.
